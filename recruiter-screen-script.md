# Recruiter Screen Script

Current as of 2026-06-01.

Purpose: prepare for first recruiter screens without overclaiming. Keep answers short, concrete, and safe.

## Target Outcome

By the end of the call, know:

- Whether the role is viable for location, timezone, remote policy, and work authorization.
- Whether the role is backend-leaning, full-stack, frontend-heavy, or platform/integrations-heavy.
- Whether the seniority bar is realistic.
- Whether the interview process is practical or heavily algorithm/FAANG-style.
- What to emphasize in the next stage.

## 30-Second Intro

I’m a backend-leaning .NET engineer with full-stack Vue/Nuxt experience, focused on SaaS workflows, integrations, background jobs, and operationally visible systems.

The strongest example is long-term independent developer/contractor work for Snagr/RDrive, where I built integration-heavy product workflows around document/data movement between RDrive and external platforms such as Aconex, Azure Blob Storage, Autodesk, and Asite.

More recently I’ve been using RefineBridge as a modern .NET, Nuxt/Vue 3, and Postgres product path, with SaaS-style concerns like auth boundaries, billing/webhook-aware flows, admin workflows, and background processing.

I’m looking for practical .NET product engineering roles where backend/integration strength matters, with room to contribute full-stack when needed.

## If Asked: Why Are You Looking?

I’ve done a lot of real product ownership in a non-standard independent setup, and I’m now packaging that experience into a more conventional hiring path. I want a stable engineering role where I can contribute to .NET product systems, keep growing in a team environment, and use my integration/workflow experience on problems that matter.

## If Asked: Current / Previous Role

Use:

> Long-term independent developer/contractor for Snagr/RDrive.

Short explanation:

I worked in a non-standard independent arrangement rather than a conventional employee ladder. The useful hiring signal is the engineering responsibility: I owned integration-heavy product work across document/data workflows, external APIs, admin tooling, background jobs, and operational visibility.

Avoid:

- Long family-friend explanation unless directly asked.
- Defensive wording.
- Claiming formal employment if not confirmed.

## If Asked: What Did RDrive DataBridge Do?

RDrive DataBridge connected RDrive with external document and data platforms. It had two main workflow types:

- Form Events: RDrive form/status events pushed documents and metadata out to configured providers.
- Synchronization: external provider document libraries were pulled back into RDrive and organized with metadata-driven folder structures.

The engineering work involved provider APIs, credentials/options, mappings, background processing, retries, health/status visibility, and admin workflows.

## If Asked: What Is RefineBridge?

RefineBridge is my current modern-stack product project. It uses .NET, Nuxt/Vue 3, and Postgres, and it lets me demonstrate current SaaS/product engineering patterns: auth/account boundaries, admin workflows, billing and webhook awareness, background jobs, integrations, and maintainable module boundaries.

Do not claim product traction unless verified separately.

## If Asked: Backend Or Full-stack?

Best answer:

I’m backend-leaning. My strongest work is .NET APIs, integrations, data/workflow modeling, background jobs, and operational visibility. But I’m comfortable shipping product-facing full-stack work, especially Vue/Nuxt and TypeScript, when the product needs it.

If role is React:

I have more Vue/Nuxt experience than React, but I’m open to React roles where the core value is .NET/product/backend work and the team is realistic about framework ramp-up.

## If Asked: Seniority

I’d describe myself as experienced and ownership-heavy rather than relying only on a title. I’ve owned substantial product/integration work end to end, but I’m also being deliberate about joining a more conventional team environment and getting feedback. So I’m open to strong mid-level, mid-to-senior, or senior roles where the expectations match practical product/backend ownership rather than formal people leadership from day one.

## If Asked: Collaboration / Solo Work

A lot of my work was independent, so I had to be clear about requirements, tradeoffs, support issues, and operational behavior. I do not want to present that as a substitute for every part of a larger engineering team. What I can bring is ownership, clear written reasoning, and a willingness to work with review, planning, and team standards.

## If Asked: Strengths

- Turning messy business workflows into configurable software.
- .NET APIs, integrations, background jobs, and data/document workflow systems.
- Provider-specific API handling and operational failure cases.
- Admin tooling and status visibility so systems can be run and supported.
- Enough frontend ability to ship complete product workflows.

## If Asked: Weaknesses / Growth Areas

Use one or two:

- I’m deliberately getting more formal interview and team-process reps because much of my work was independent.
- I have stronger Vue/Nuxt experience than React, though I can ramp if the backend/product fit is right.
- I want more production exposure in a conventional team with code review, shared standards, and broader engineering feedback.

Do not say:

- "I have no experience."
- "I only worked alone."
- "I am not senior."

## Questions To Ask Recruiter

Ask the most relevant 5-7, not all:

- Is this role remote from anywhere, remote within a country, remote within a region, or hybrid?
- What work authorization is required?
- What timezone overlap is required?
- Is the role more backend/platform, full-stack product, or frontend-heavy?
- Which stack is non-negotiable: .NET, cloud, React/Vue, SQL, domain experience?
- What seniority expectations matter most: delivery ownership, mentoring, architecture, stakeholder communication, or people leadership?
- What does the interview process include?
- Is there a live coding, take-home, system design, or project walkthrough stage?
- Is the coding interview practical product/API work or algorithm-heavy?
- What is the salary/rate range?
- What would make someone fail this role despite having .NET experience?

## Red Flags

- Remote policy unclear or changed after the first call.
- "Worldwide remote" but payroll/work authorization is country-specific.
- Senior role expects formal people leadership from day one.
- Heavy algorithm screen for a practical .NET/product role, unless deliberately accepted.
- Role is actually frontend-first but uses ".NET" lightly.
- No salary/rate range after reasonable recruiter discussion.
- Pressure to disclose private client/live-system details.
