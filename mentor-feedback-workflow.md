# Mentor Feedback Workflow

Current as of 2026-06-02.

Purpose: get targeted outside feedback on Richard's hiring story, technical explanation, role fit, and interview delivery without asking vague "can you mentor me?" questions.

This workflow assumes Richard may not already have specific contacts. Start from reviewer categories, then use professional networks, existing acquaintances, paid mock sessions, relevant technical communities, or warm introductions only after verifying the person/service is current and appropriate.

## Operating Principles

- Ask for one narrow review, not ongoing mentorship.
- Make the request answerable in 20-30 minutes.
- Send a small packet: context, exact question, artifact link/text, and desired feedback format.
- Do not ask reviewers to rewrite everything.
- Do not sound desperate, apologetic, or overexplain the Snagr/RDrive arrangement.
- Do not send raw RDrive screenshots, live URLs, client names, project IDs, usernames, credentials, or private customer data.
- Treat feedback as evidence, not truth. One opinion is a signal; repeated feedback becomes a decision.
- Every feedback session must produce one logged note and one next action.

## Reviewer Types

| Reviewer type | Best use | Ask them to evaluate | Do not ask them for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Senior engineer | Technical credibility, architecture explanation, reliability depth, team-readiness framing | Whether the RDrive DataBridge or RefineBridge explanation sounds technically credible for .NET/product engineering roles | Broad career rescue, job leads before fit is clear, or private code review without a tight question |
| Hiring manager/recruiter | Role fit, seniority calibration, CV/LinkedIn clarity, screening risks | Whether the profile reads as backend .NET, full-stack .NET product, integrations/platform, or another bucket | Deep technical architecture review or generic "what job should I get?" |
| Founder/product person | Business value, product narrative, customer/problem clarity, RefineBridge optionality | Whether the case studies explain practical product value without overclaiming traction | Formal interview scoring or detailed .NET implementation critique |
| Mock interviewer | Interview pressure, concise delivery, answer structure, follow-up handling | Whether answers are clear under time pressure and where Richard rambles/freezes | Long-term mentoring, job-search strategy, or rewriting CV from scratch |

## Feedback Cadence

Run this as a lightweight loop.

1. Pick one target question.
2. Pick one reviewer type.
3. Send one low-friction request.
4. Prepare a tiny review packet.
5. Log the feedback within 24 hours.
6. Update the affected artifacts.
7. Add one practice action.

Suggested pace:
- Week 1: 2-3 narrow asks.
- Weeks 2-4: 1 ask per week, plus one mock interview or recorded answer review.
- After first applications: use recruiter/hiring-manager feedback to adjust role targeting and public materials.

## Review Packets

Use the smallest packet that can answer the question.

### CV / LinkedIn Fit Packet

Send:
- 3-line context.
- `cv-draft.md` excerpt: summary, skills, Snagr/RDrive role, RefineBridge role.
- `linkedin-profile-draft.md` headline and About section.
- Exact question: "What role family would you screen this for, and what would make you hesitate?"

### RDrive DataBridge Technical Packet

Send:
- `rdrive-databridge-case-study.md` 30-second explanation.
- Product Problem section.
- System Shape section.
- Exact question: "Does this sound like credible integration/platform engineering experience, and what would you probe in an interview?"

### RefineBridge Product/Modern Stack Packet

Send:
- `refinebridge-case-study.md` 30-second explanation.
- Claim Boundary section.
- System Shape section.
- Exact question: "Does this work as a modern-stack proof point without sounding like unsupported traction?"

### Interview Delivery Packet

Send or use live:
- 30-second intro.
- One 2-minute project explanation.
- One answer from `behavioral-story-bank.md`.
- Exact question: "Where did the answer become unclear, too long, or unconvincing?"

## First 3 Recommended Feedback Asks

Do these before broad networking or expensive coaching.

### Ask 1: Role-Fit Calibration

Reviewer type: hiring manager/recruiter.

Question:
"Would you screen this profile as backend .NET, full-stack .NET product, integrations/platform, or something else? What would make you hesitate?"

Packet:
- CV Professional Summary.
- Core Skills.
- Snagr/RDrive experience section.
- LinkedIn headline and About section.

Output needed:
- One recommended role-family label.
- Top 2 screening risks.
- One suggested wording change.

Why first:
This tests the public story before applications and prevents over-optimizing the wrong title.

### Ask 2: RDrive DataBridge Technical Credibility

Reviewer type: senior engineer.

Question:
"Does this sound like credible integration/platform engineering experience for .NET roles? What technical details would you expect me to defend in an interview?"

Packet:
- RDrive 30-second explanation.
- Product Problem.
- System Shape.
- Operational Reliability.

Output needed:
- Strongest technical proof point.
- Weakest or vaguest claim.
- 3 likely interview follow-up questions.

Why second:
RDrive DataBridge is the strongest commercial case study and needs to hold up under technical probing.

### Ask 3: Mock Delivery Baseline

Reviewer type: mock interviewer.

Question:
"Where does my delivery break down: structure, confidence, concision, technical depth, or follow-up answers?"

Packet:
- 30-second intro.
- 2-minute RDrive walkthrough.
- One behavioral story about solo ownership or ambiguous requirements.

Output needed:
- Score each answer 0-4 using the weak-spot tracker scale.
- Identify the single biggest delivery issue.
- Assign one practice rep for the next week.

Why third:
Richard's biggest unknown is not only content quality; it is performance under interview pressure.

## How Feedback Updates The System

Every feedback item should update one or more existing files. Do not let feedback become untracked advice.

### CV Updates

Update `cv-draft.md` when feedback shows:
- the target role label is unclear
- the professional summary does not match likely screening buckets
- bullets are too generic, too inflated, or too implementation-heavy
- RDrive/RefineBridge claims need stronger boundaries
- a reviewer identifies a missing proof point that belongs in a bullet

Rule:
Change CV wording only when the feedback improves screening clarity or removes a credibility risk. Do not rewrite the whole CV from one opinion.

### LinkedIn Updates

Update `linkedin-profile-draft.md` when feedback shows:
- the headline is not mapping to the intended roles
- About section is too long, apologetic, or unclear
- recruiter keywords are missing or misleading
- featured items need safer labels or a better order

Rule:
LinkedIn should stay readable and recruiter-friendly. Avoid turning it into a dense case study.

### Story Updates

Update `behavioral-story-bank.md` when feedback shows:
- a story lacks a concrete problem, constraint, decision, tradeoff, or result
- a reviewer asks a follow-up Richard cannot answer
- a story sounds defensive about solo work
- the business value is too vague
- the answer overclaims traction, formal title, or implementation certainty

Rule:
Each story update should add one confirmed detail, one missing question, or one sharper answer outline. Do not invent facts to satisfy a reviewer.

### Weak-Spot Updates

Update `weak-spot-tracker.md` when feedback shows:
- a weak spot score should rise or fall based on actual performance
- a new weak spot appears
- priority changes because a reviewer repeats a concern
- the next practice action should change

Rule:
Raise scores only after output improves. Feedback alone can lower a score or change priority, but it should not raise confidence without a successful rep.

### Practice Plan Updates

Update the current practice plan or practice log when feedback shows:
- a mock interview exposed a repeated failure mode
- a technical explanation needs drill work
- a story needs spoken reps
- a CV/LinkedIn risk should be tested in outreach
- a role-family assumption needs market validation

Rule:
Every feedback session should create one practice action small enough to finish within a week.

## Decision Rules

Use these rules to avoid overreacting.

| Signal | Action |
| --- | --- |
| One reviewer dislikes wording but understands the story | Log it; optional wording tweak |
| Two reviewer types flag the same unclear claim | Update CV/LinkedIn/story wording |
| Senior engineer flags a technical gap | Add interview follow-up questions and a technical drill |
| Recruiter/hiring reviewer maps profile to unexpected role family | Revisit `positioning-v1.md` and `target-role-matrix.md` |
| Mock interviewer scores delivery 0-1 | Add spoken practice before more artifact polishing |
| Founder/product reviewer cannot see business value | Rewrite case-study result/business-value section |
| Reviewer asks for private proof | Do not share raw private evidence; create a sanitized summary instead |

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:
- "Can you mentor me?"
- "Can I pick your brain?"
- "Any advice for my career?"
- "Here is my whole life story."
- "Please review my CV, LinkedIn, case studies, GitHub, and interview answers."
- "I am desperate to get hired."
- "I have no network and need help."
- Sending unredacted screenshots or private RDrive/customer details.

Use instead:
- "Could you answer one fit question?"
- "Could you give 3 bullets on what sounds credible, unclear, and risky?"
- "Could you tell me what role family this profile maps to?"
- "Could you score this answer and give one next practice action?"

## Placeholders To Fill Before Sending Requests

- Exact public name and contact details.
- LinkedIn URL.
- GitHub or portfolio URL, if ready.
- Location/timezone wording.
- Work authorization / contracting constraints.
- Preferred role-family wording for the specific ask.
- Safe public links for RDrive/RefineBridge, if any.
- Whether the reviewer can receive private notes or only public-safe material.

## Manual Review Checklist

Before each request:
- The ask can be answered in 20-30 minutes.
- The reviewer type matches the question.
- The packet is short enough to read quickly.
- The exact question is visible in the message.
- Private RDrive/RefineBridge details are removed.
- The message does not ask for ongoing mentorship.
- The expected output is concrete: role label, risks, follow-up questions, score, or next action.

After each request:
- Feedback is logged.
- One artifact update is made or deliberately deferred.
- One practice action is created.
- Any repeated signal is noted.
- Any unsupported claim is removed or marked for confirmation.
